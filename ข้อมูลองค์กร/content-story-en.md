### History of the Office of Military Education (OME)

General Pornpipat Benyasri, commander in hief, issued a policy to establish an additional education unit responsible for training and education in order to continuously and concretely develop Headquarters, Royal Thai Armed Forces personnel for joint operations and professional soldiering.

To implement this policy, General Narain Siripuban, Commandant of the National Defence Studies Institute (NDSI), signed NDSI Order 11/2019 on 6 June 2019 assigning officers to pilot the Office of Military Education (OME) from 6 June to 30 September 2019 and to conduct the first Senior NCO course. On 10 July 2019, then-Defence Minister General Prawit Wongsuwan approved the establishment of the “Office of Military Education” under the NDSI, effective 1 October 2019.

OME was officially established on 1 October 2019 under TOP-SECRET Thai Armed Forces Headquarters Order 209/62 dated 25 July 2019. Its missions include organising military-service-oriented training courses, managing domestic and overseas education staff work, fostering academic cooperation with domestic and international agencies, disseminating military science, and operating the military library. The office comprises four divisions—Education, Training, Science, and Support—and its first Director was Maj. Gen. Paisal Ngamwongwan.
